Fake Blakes

This exhibition comprises seven galleries of William Blake’s illuminated books and prints that were not printed or colored by William Blake. The first six galleries identify copies of illuminated books that were printed after Blake died in 1827, either by Catherine Blake or Frederick Tatham, Blake’s follower who inherited the illuminated plates after Catherine died in October 1831. All of them were finished in water colors long after Catherine and Tatham died, either in the late 19th or early 20th century, and sold as life time copies.

  • Gallery 2: Songs of Innocence and of Experience copy e/K
